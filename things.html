Things to do

Outdoor Activities

Hiking
Scotland is perfect for hiking, with lots of walking paths available - from easy hikes for the whole family to strenous mountain climbs. There are 282 Munros (mountains over 3000 feet) to choose from, forest walks and coastal paths.

Mountain Biking
Whether you're a beginner or an experienced cyclist, Scotland is one of the best places for mountain biking. From cycling trails to more rugged off-road tracks, there are plenty of beautiful landscapes for you to explore on two wheels.
Watersports
Scotland has over 30,000 lakes (known as lochs), 10,000km of coastline and many rivers - making it the perfect place to enjoy watersports. You can try surfing, kayaking, paddleboarding, rafting, sailing and much more.

Adrenaline Activities
Scotland has plenty of adventure to offer thrill-seekers - with a wide range of exciting activies like bungee jumping, paragliding, kitesurfing, white water rafting and microlighting, you'll find something to get your heart racing.

History

Ancient Ruins & Sites

Scotland is steeped in history and legend and has a wealth of fascinating historical sites, some of which date back to the earliest inhabitants of the British isles. You'll find standing stones and burial cairns, Neolithic villages and prehistoric settlements, and much more!


Castles

Scotland is famous for its castles, and there are hundreds dotting the country. Varying from ruins to well-preserved fortresses, each castle has a rich history and often a scenic backdrop. You may even recognise some from films and TV shows - for example Game of Thrones, James Bond and Outlander.


Monuments & Landmarks

Scotland has many attractions showcasing it's rich and proud history. Some of the best to visit include the Glenfinnan Viaduct - famous for its role in the Harry Potter films, The National Wallace Monument - commemorating Scottish hero William Wallace and the Forth Bridge - one of the world's longest railway bridges.
